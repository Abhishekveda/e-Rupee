## Government of India Channels

### 5. Ministry of Finance — Department of Economic Affairs

DEA oversees India's international financial architecture, including BRICS financial cooperation and INR internationalisation.

**Portal:** https://dea.gov.in  
**Contact form:** https://dea.gov.in/contact-us  
**Email:** webmaster@dea.gov.in (for general enquiries — ask for the FinTech desk)

**Why they care:** The INR bridge currency concept directly serves India's stated policy of reducing dollar dependency and promoting rupee internationalisation. The BRICS CBDC interconnection proposal (for the 2026 summit) is a DEA-level initiative, not just an RBI initiative.

**Subject line:**
```
Open-Source INR Bridge Currency Protocol — BRICS CBDC Interoperability
```

### 6. MeitY — Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology

MeitY funds India's digital infrastructure and runs the India AI Mission.

**Portal:** https://www.meity.gov.in  
**Email:** webmaster@meity.gov.in  
**India AI Mission:** https://indiaai.gov.in  
**What to apply for:** India AI Mission startup grants for AI-powered fintech infrastructure

**Key connection:** Your custom AI agent qualifies as an Indian AI project under the India AI Mission. The 5-agent system, trained on RBI regulatory data, is exactly what the mission was designed to support.

### 7. NPCI — National Payments Corporation of India

NPCI operates UPI, RuPay, and IMPS. They are the natural technology partner for any RBI-adjacent payment infrastructure project.

**Website:** https://www.npci.org.in  
**Innovation email:** innovation@npci.org.in  
**LinkedIn contact:**
- Dilip Asbe — MD & CEO, NPCI
- Search: "Dilip Asbe NPCI" on LinkedIn

**Why they care:** NPCI is actively expanding UPI internationally and building India's cross-border payment infrastructure. Your INR bridge protocol is a natural complement to their international UPI work.

### 8. IFSCA — International Financial Services Centres Authority (GIFT City)

GIFT City is India's designated international financial centre. IFSCA has a mandate to attract fintech companies and build cross-border financial infrastructure.

**Portal:** https://ifsca.gov.in  
**Email:** helpdesk@ifsca.gov.in  
**FinTech incentives:** https://ifsca.gov.in/FinTech

**Why relevant:** Deploying e₹ Bridge in GIFT City's IFSC would allow international companies to use INR settlement without Indian regulatory friction. IFSCA has specifically invited cross-border payment innovations.

### 9. Startup India

Register your project on the Startup India portal for visibility with Government departments and potential grant access.

**Portal:** https://www.startupindia.gov.in  
**Register as:** Financial Technology / Payments  
**Benefits:** DIPP recognition, tax exemptions, fast-track IPR, government tender eligibility

## The one thing that matters most

Every communication should end with the same sentence:

> "The code is open source, the demo is live, and I can run it in front of you on a laptop in 30 seconds. When would you like to see it?"

That offer — live, in-person, on a laptop, 30 seconds — is what closes meetings. Most applicants send PDFs. You run working software. That is the difference.
